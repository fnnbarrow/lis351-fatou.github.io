Rhythm and Blues

First originating and performed by African American artists, RnB or R&B is one of the most popular music genres in the United States and recently across the world. RnB stands for Rhythm and Blues which not only describes the elements of the genre but also the origination. RnB combines elements such as Jazz, Gospel, and Blues as influences as well as typical instruments such as a guitar, bass, saxophone, drum kit, and keyboard that make up it as a genre. The term "Rhythm and Blues" has been used as an umbrella term to "refer to any music made by and for Black Americans". It is also used a term used to describe how RnB is a genre that uses a certain style of music that uses a 12 bar blues format and boogie-woogie with a back beat, though this has since evolved. The original rhythm and blues genre first came about in the 1940s, gaining traction in the 1950s in places such as New Orleans. Many groups such as The Rolling Stones and the Yardbirds were considered RnB bands at the start of their careers in the 1960s.

These are the some of the sub genres that are included in and known as an extension of the RnB genre:
Classic Rhythm and Blues
Defined by various characteristics such as piano trios, vocal harmonies, and rhythm and horn sections
Contemporary RnB
Includes hip-hop production techniques and sampling, with the absence of real instruments. Groups such as SWV, TLC, Destiny's Child and En Vogue dominated charts with the comtemporary RnB sound
New Jack Swing
Developed and introduced by Teddy Riley and Bernard Belle, "originated from combining hip-hop producutions with dance and contemporary RnB sound". Later into what we know as the 90s RnB sound.
Disco
Helped create a more dancable form of RnB
Doo Wop
Funk
Famous artists of this sub genre are Stevie Wonder, Diana Ross, The Jackson 5, Chaka Khan, and the godfather of funk: James brown
Motown
Quiet Storm
Soul
Adopted first bu artists such as Ray Charles, Marvin Gate, Aretha Franklin, and The Supremes.
Neo-Soul
Rose to popularity in the 2000s from emerging artists such as Alicia Keys and Amy Winehouse. Includes live instrument recordings.
